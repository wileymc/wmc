Address: 2322 N Marion Street, Denver CO 80205

## Getting Here
From Denver International Airport, you can take the A line towards Union Station.  The closest light rail station is 38th and Blake Street Station.  Walking from here takes about 30 minutes but its a very quick uber or scooter ride.  There are always scooters and bikes available at this station.

## Parking
We have ample street parking on Marion street.  **Street Sweeping** happens April - November, on the second Tuesday and Wednesday of the month.  Read the signs carefully and sign up for the cities text alert system to avoid getting ticketed. 

## Access to the House
- The front door code is 7602.  
When you leave, please lock the front door by hitting the "Slage" button on the top of the lock box outside.  You'll hear the deadbolt slide and then can test that the door is indeed locked.  The back doors are typically unlocked, but if you would like to lock them, you can do so by turning the handle so that the handle is facing 12 o'clock, then turn the locking knob below.  These unlock when you open them from the inside.  

## Garage

If you use the garage, please ensure that the large garage door is closed and that the access door is also locked.  Clicking the lock icon on the keypad will automatically close the deadbolt. You can use the blue and pink bicycle if you desire. Please ask before using other bicycles.

## Kitchen
- Feel free to use anything in the fridge or pantry.
- Beverage fridge around the corner to the left of the refrigerator
- Pots and pans in the lower cabinet to the left of the stove
- Spices and cooking utilities in the upper cabinet to the left of the microwave
- Please spray and wipe down the stove after every use
- Cutlery should be washed, dried, and returned to the magnetic rack immediately after use
- **Dishes should be washed immediately after cooking**, please leave the sink clear for the next group using the kitchen.  
	- There is a black squeegee that can be used to clear food particles from the sink.
	- Switch for the garbage disposal located on the left hand side of the sink
	- Dish soap and dishwashing pods located under the sink
- **Hand Towels**: we use hand towels instead of paper.  Find them in the lower cabinet to the right hand side of the kitchen sink.  Dirty towels go in the grocery basket beneath the pantry to the right of the stove.  Please launder these if you see them running low.
- **Vacuum** each night if you use the kitchen.  There is a Kirby that works great.  Step on the large arrows on the left of the machine to drop the headset to the floor, then click the big ON/OFF button on the back and make sure the switch is set to "D" otherwise its tough to push.
#### Dishwasher
- Please no cast iron or copper pots in dishwasher 
- Dishwashing pods located under the sink
- The dishwasher works great, so long as you load it correctly.  
	- Glassware and small bowls on top rack, plates and larger items on bottom rack
	- Face dishes towards the center, angle them downward, and avoid overcrowding

#### Espresso Machine
- Feel free to make yourself a coffee whatever way you take it.  The machine is semi-automatic meaning that you'll still need to fill, press and pull your own shot. 
- Hold the on/off button for 3 seconds to turn the machine on
- This machine takes approximately 15 minutes to warm up when first turned on.  A solid yellow light will be visible when the machine is at temperature.  If you want to steam milk, ensure the boiler is on by pressing the button with the steam icon on the far left, a red light will appear over the boiler indicator that will be solid when the machine is ready to steam.
- A [full video tutorial can be found here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wV-dmqrSLak) if you're unsure about how to use this machine.
- Please clear your grounds out of the portafilter after use.  You can discard your pucks into the sliding drawer beneath the grinder to the left of the machine.  These should be composted in the green bin in the kitchen or directly outside in the alleyway.

## Laundry
- Laundry is located in the basement, at the foot of the stairs.  Detergent on top of machine.

## Wood Stove
- Wood can be found on the patio beside the rear gate to the alley.  There is a carrying apparatus that lives near the stove
- Please clear out ash from stove before starting new fire, use shovel and ash bucket to left of stove
- Use small kindling to get fire started
- Please be sparing in your use of paper products to get the fire started, this clogs the chimney and inhibits airflow
- Airflow can be controlled with lever on right hand side of stove, lever up == good for starting, burns faster.  Lever down == once fire started, slow and steady flames

## Getting Around
The neighborhood is very walkable and even more bike-able.  We have a spare bicycle in the garage if you'd like to ride.  There is a keyed padlock and coil that you can use to lock the bike up when you park.  

## The Neighborhood
We are in a beautiful and historical neighborhood called Whittier.  At the top of the block there is a primary school and a community garden.  You can use this field for exercise, dog walking, and more when school is not in session (8am-3pm Aug-May)

City Park is approximately 0.7 miles to the east and is a lovely walk down 21nd or 24th St.  There are lots of tennis courts here and fun events on the weekends during summer months:
- City Park Farmers market Saturdays April - October near East High School
- Jazz in the Park: Sundays June - September (check the schedule online)

River North (RiNo) is a nearby neighborhood with lots of restaurants, bars, and small businesses.  It is a quick walk from the house.
